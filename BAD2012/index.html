Teach a man to fish and you'll feed him for a day, teach a child to think and she'll grow up to enact legislation that limits overfishing and saves our resources for future generations.

The idea behind this website and poster was to try and make the world a more rational place by helping to educate people, especially older kids, in critical thinking and logical fallacies.

The existing resources for logical fallacies online were written for an academic audience, effectively preaching to the choir.

We wanted to create an online resource that explained common logical fallacies in language that a ten year old could understand, and with an interface that was simple and intuitive. Being able to identify common logical fallacies is not only an engaging way to get kids interested in philosophy and critical thinking, it’s also very effective at immunising them from the bullshit and spin that they’re exposed to from politicians, the media, and bigots.

To be relevant to the context in which it would be applied, we bought the domain yourlogicalfallacyis.com so that users could reply in the comments sections of Facebook posts, forums or news articles with a link to the fallacy that someone had committed e.g. if your homophobic uncle wrote a post saying how gay marriage would lead to polygamy and people marrying their cars, you could simply reply with a link to yourlogicalfallacyis.com/slippery-slope

To make the concept maximally shareable, we also created a logical fallacy infographic poster and published it on the site under a creative commons licence.

The site was written in html5 to allow for clean and simple animation that didn't require additional plugins or codecs.

Results

Since launching just over 3 months ago, the site has had 1,115,343 visitors (915,898 unique), who spent an average of 4.11 minutes on the site.

On its best day the site had 65,625 unique visitors, and is still averaging between 5,000 - 10,000 unique visitors per day.

In the first month we clocked up a total of 198GB of bandwidth.

Despite there being a number of sites that had well over a decade of tenure, yourlogicalfallacyis.com is now in the top 5 results of an organic google search for 'logical fallacies' worldwide, and is being linked to by over 3,600 domains - in only 3 months.

But the highest hope we had for this project would be that it would be hung in children’s bedrooms and used by teachers all around the world. So far the poster has been downloaded over 250,000 times, and the site and its materials are being incorporated into school curriculums everywhere, helping to make the world an altogether thinkier place.
